To begin, The training data has 14 parameters, some more useful here than others.

pt is our transverse momentum, mass is the jet mass. These two will most likely be the most useful.

We have angularity,eta,phi, and ktdeltaR. All of these are spacial measurements usful in reconstructing the path of the jet and particles.

The t parameters seem to be related to a kind of jet shape that is usful at distinguishing between multiple differebt kidns of particles

ee2,ee3, and d2 are related to energy correlation and are usful when deeling with hadronic decays and Z boson detection

1.Do all features provide discrimination power between signal and background?

Some parameters discriminate between signal and background very dramaticly others are almost indistinguishable and therefor not very usful to us.

Some notable parameters include:

Mass and pt, both of which have a very distinct spike in the signal data that looks nothing like the background.

ee2,t2,t3,t21,ktdeltaR all have fairly differentiable signals but not to the degree that mass and pt are differentiable. These ar characterized more by a shift in the weight of the distrabution more than any change in amplitude.

eta,phi,d2,ee3,angularity,t1, and t32 all have signals that look a bit too much like the background distrabution to be very useful, some signals (like ee3) dont even make it out of the background distrabutions amplitude or range.

2.Are there correlations among these features?

The few obvous corrilations we can see are mostly in the shape of the distrabution. pt and mass are spikes in the mid-higher ranges, the tx and txx signals are all psuedo-exponential curves leaning left (or right for t32). ee3, d2, and angularity are all very near zero.

  1. below are computations for the discovery senstivity for a mass and pt measurement.

To begin, on the left there is a plot of both signal and background. I used this to pick a small x-range in which the background and signal vary significantly different. This can be seen in the second (middle) plot. I limited the histogram x axis to 483-486, and set the bins to 1, which gave me the total number of events in that region. Since this is simmulated data, I assumed it was a resonable average event number. I used the total background events in the range 483-486 as my average when caluculting and plotting my poisson. Using this poisson I found the 5 sigma limit. Which in the case of pt (in the range 483-486) was 2544 events.

fig, ax = plt.subplots(1,1)
mu = 2300
x = np.arange(poisson.ppf(0.000001, mu),poisson.ppf(0.999999, mu))
ax.plot(x, poisson.pmf(x, mu))

sig = poisson.ppf((norm.cdf(5)),mu)
print("5 sigma detection boundry =",sig)
